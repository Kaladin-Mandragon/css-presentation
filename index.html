CSS Topic - Spacing

The letter-spacing and word-spacing properties allow for the addition and the removal of whitespace between letters and words respectively. They are able to be used together to mess with both the whitespace between letters and the whitespace between words. If you do not use either of these then they are defaulted to letter-spacing: normal; and word-spacing: normal; respectively. They are both inheritable and can use pixels to change the amount of whitespace.

See the Pen No-Spacing by Kaladin-Mandragon (@kaladin-mandragon) on CodePen.

Spacing Between Letters

To change the amount of whitespace between individual letters you use letter-spacing: 10px to add 10 pixels of whitespace. One thing to note is that you can also use negative numbers to decrease the amount of whitespace between letters. One thing to keep an eye on is how easy it is to read your text after adding or removing a certain amount of whitespace. Make sure that your text is still readable unless it was your goal to make it illegible.

See the Pen Letter Spacing by Kaladin-Mandragon (@kaladin-mandragon) on CodePen.

Spacing Between Words

To change the amount of whitespace between different words you use word-spacing: 15px to add 15 pixels of whitespace. You can also use negative numbers to remove whitespace between words however just like with the spacing between letters there is no limit to the amount of whitespace that can be added or removed. If you add too much or remove too much it becomes difficult and sometimes impossible to read.
